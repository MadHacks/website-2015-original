About MadHacks

What

MadHacks 2015 is UW-Madison's first intercollegiate national hackathon. Come make history with 300 of your peers in one of the Midwest's greatest collaborative events this spring.

Where

MadHacks will be hosted at 605 Science Drive, Madison, WI. We're in an abandoned-laboratory-turned-hackerspace this year — how fitting!

Who

You! If you're 18+ and in school, you should apply to MadHacks to come showcase your talents! Students of all backgrounds and skill levels are welcome!

How

You'll spend 24 hours hacking on a new project in teams of 1-4. At the end of it all, you'll demo your project to the other hackers. In the meantime, you'll have free food, enjoy activities such as: a costume contest, dance competition, and gaming in our dedicated leisure lounge; meet wonderful sponsors, and make lifelong friends. May the maddest project win!

What's a Hackathon?

If you've never had the pleasure of attending a hackathon before, check out this post about what a hackathon is and why you should attend one!

Be sure to review the MLH code of conduct to know what to expect at MadHacks!

What makes MadHacks different?

Unlike other hackathons, MadHacks is going to be putting emphasis on the wacky, the original, and the downright impossible! Like the mad geniuses, tinkerers, makers, and designers preceding us, we want to test our boundaries like never before! We're reaching out to the highly dedicated risk-takers, the envelope pushers, and the daredevils. We hope to produce a new kind of hackathon: where the winners are chosen by the hackers, and the sponsors feel like they are right in the middle of the hacker community itself — rather than on the outside looking in.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who can attend?

You must be at least 18 years old to attend MadHacks, unless you are a volunteer from a local high school.

Is MadHacks free?

Yep! If you're accepted, you'll be attending MadHacks for free.

Will there be mentors at MadHacks?

Yes - we will have plenty of resources for first-time hackers, including mentors from top employers. Be sure to visit their offices and ask questions!

What's the food situation?

MadHacks will be catered with enough food to keep you hacking well into the night, thanks to our wonderful sponsors. In our registration form, please indicate any dietary restrictions you have so we are able to accommodate you as we attempt create orders.

If I drive, where can I park?

The wonderful thing about our venue is we can offer you free parking, while supplies last! Please indicate this in your application so we know what the demand is like.

How can I find a carpool?

If you wish to provide a ride to hackers in your area, please contact team@madhacks.org — if you're looking to join a carpool, please indicate this on the RSVP form.

Will there be buses or travel reimbursement?

We have included travel costs into our budget, but routes and reimbursements will be dependent on demand, as well as pricing. It's likely that we'll be sending buses along major routes for nearby (Midwest) colleges. Please indicate your preference for travel on your application to help us better determine allocations.

What kind of hardware is available to hack with?

You can always bring your own hardware along to hack, but we'll have some available to check out for the weekend! Here's a sample of what we've got:

  • 6x Arduino
  • 6x Breadboards
  • 3x Dell Monitors
  • 10x Leap Motion
  • 3x Muse
  • 19x Myo
  • 6x Oculus Rift
  • 15x Pebble
  • 6x Sparkcore
  • 1x Galileo board

Hardware is supply-limited, so be sure to check it out early if you're interested!

How can I form my team?

If you do not have a team and would like to form one prior to the event, consider posting in the MadHacks Participants Group.

There will also be a team forming session near the beginning of the hackathon.

Will there be fun activities at MadHacks?

Of course! We've got plans for some or all of the following things:

  • Tag
  • Frisbee
  • Costume content
  • Mafia
  • Water balloon fights
  • Video games
  • Board games
  • Card games
  • Trivia

What should I bring?

Whatever you need to make your hack a success! For most people, this means a laptop, charger, smartphone, and your friends! Optional, but nice things include: a pillow, blanket or sleeping bag, a change of clothes, a toothbrush, toothpaste, and deodorant. Please don't bring anything you wouldn't take on a school field trip.

Our venue also has a single shower, so feel free to bring your own soap and don't forget to bring a towel.

How will my hack be judged?

Fellow hackers will be your judge during the expo! This means that a voting system will be in place for you to choose the MadHacks winners. There will be prizes for the most mad hack, best hardware, best software, and the most complicated way to accomplish a mundane task, etc.

There will also be API prizes provided by our corporate sponsors. If you wish to compete for these prizes, please indicate this when submitting your project to ChallengePost.

What are the prizes?

We will be releasing detailed prize information from MadHacks and sponsors closer to the event.

How big can my team be?

Team sizes may be from 1 to 4 members.

Where's the event relative to the UW campus? How can I get there?

The hackathon will be taking place at 605 Science Drive. Take a look at the following map for an idea of where the location is with respect to the UW campus.

Getting to MadHacks

  • If you're coming from the east, consider taking Badger Bus — it's $20 one-way.
  • If coming from the south, west, or north, consider Van Galder Bus Lines or Greyhound.
  • Most major bus lines will drop you off near downtown Madison.
  • There is an airport in downtown Madison if you are flying in, but consider flying into Milwaukee and taking the Badger Bus to Madison. This is the cheaper option.
  • If these above routes do not work, we are also arranging carpools. Please indicate your preference on the RSVP form.
  • If you are coming from downtown #2, #6, and #7 bus lines from the Madison Metro Transit System run directly through downtown to our venue and back. All bus lines cost $2 and will run from approximately 6:30 am CT to midnight CT. Google Maps has information on the bus routes.
